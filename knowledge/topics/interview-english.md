# Interview English

## Goal
- Improve English specifically for interviews, especially job interviews.
- Focus on speaking clearly and reliably, not sounding fancy.

## Core principle
- Do not try to "learn all English" first.
- Practice interview English by scenario: self-introduction, project explanation, motivation, behavior questions, and recovery phrases.

## Priority areas
1. Self-introduction (1-minute and 2-minute versions)
2. Project explanation
3. Why this role / why this company
4. Behavioral questions
5. Recovery / clarification phrases

## Useful structure for project answers
- What it was
- Why you did it
- What your role was
- What you made / how you approached it
- What happened / what you learned

## Useful sentence stems
- I have a background in...
- I'm interested in...
- My project focused on...
- My role was...
- One challenge was...
- What I learned from that experience was...
- I'm now trying to move into...
- I'm especially interested in this role because...

## Recovery phrases
- Sorry, could you say that again?
- Just to make sure I understood correctly...
- Let me think for a second.
- I may not explain it perfectly, but...
- What I mean is...
- I don't have direct experience with that yet, but...

## Notes
- Important AI terminology can remain in English.
- Favor simple, stable, speakable English over complex wording.
- Spoken practice matters more than silent reading.
